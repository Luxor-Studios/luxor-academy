L6 — Categorical Proofs

// Tier: Master · Source: fstar-framework · Level 6/7

01Primer

Involutions, functoriality, naturality — proved in F*

Level 6 is where the 7-level framework earns its categorical name. F* types form a cartesian-closed category: objects are types, morphisms are total functions, products are pairs, exponentials are function types a -> b. Every pure function is a morphism; every polymorphic function is a natural transformation between functors; every self-inverse function is an involution, i.e., a morphism f : A -> A satisfying f ∘ f = id_A.

The Week 2 flagship proof is exactly that statement for reverse. An involution is a morphism whose square is the identity. In F*:

let rec reverse (#a:Type) (xs:list a) : list a =
  match xs with
  | [] -> []
  | hd :: tl -> append (reverse tl) [hd]

val reverse_reverse: #a:Type -> xs:list a ->
  Lemma (reverse (reverse xs) == xs)

The proof is not a one-liner. It decomposes into two helpers — reverse_append (which is naturality of reverse with respect to append: reverse (xs ++ ys) == reverse ys ++ reverse xs) and reverse_singleton — and then recurses structurally. Each helper is itself a lemma, and each lemma's return value is () because a lemma proves, it does not compute. The 'output' is the knowledge F* gains.

Read the decomposition categorically and the pattern snaps into focus. reverse is a contravariant operation on the List functor; reverse_append is the statement that reverse is a natural transformation from List a x List b to List b x List a; and reverse_reverse is the claim that squaring this natural transformation yields the identity. F* does not let you write functor, natural transformation, or adjoint directly — but the proofs of their laws are exactly the shape you just wrote. This is L6: the categorical structure is latent, the proofs are explicit, and F* + Z3 connect the dots.

At L6 the categorical laws are provable statements in F*'s logic: reverse is an involution iff reverse_reverse typechecks, and the proof decomposes naturally into the naturality lemma reverse_append plus structural induction.

05Self-Check

1 — Why is the body of every lemma in F* just () (unit)?
Lemmas **prove** propositions, they do not compute values. The interesting content is in the *type* — Lemma (reverse (reverse xs) == xs) — and the () is a marker that the proof obligations introduced by the type have been discharged. The solutions file names this directly: 'the real output is the knowledge F* gains about the property being true.'
2 — Why is reverse_append (the statement that reverse (xs ++ ys) == reverse ys ++ reverse xs) required as a helper before the main involution proof?
In the inductive case hd :: tl of reverse_reverse, expanding reverse (reverse (hd :: tl)) yields reverse (append (reverse tl) [hd]). The SMT solver does not know how reverse distributes over append, so without the helper lemma the subsequent rewriting step stalls. The helper is naturality of reverse-over-append, and it is exactly the bridge F* needs.
3 — Read categorically: the statement reverse_reverse : reverse (reverse xs) == xs says that reverse is an involution on list a. Which of these is an equivalent categorical claim?
An involution is a morphism f : A -> A whose square is the identity: f ∘ f = id_A. For reverse : list a -> list a, that is exactly reverse ∘ reverse == id_{list a}, which — evaluated pointwise — is reverse (reverse xs) == xs. The F* proof and the categorical claim are the same statement in two notations.
