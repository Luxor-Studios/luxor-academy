01Primer

Calibrate before you commit

MERCURIO the PRAGMATIST is the sub-agent whose entire job is to refuse to ship decisions that look good on paper but collapse on contact with real budgets, real team bandwidth, and real calendars. The agent file calls it the master of navigating reality while maintaining vision and organizes its work across three dimensions: Reality (seeing what actually is), Navigation (finding pragmatic paths), and Wisdom (keeping perspective on what matters).

The Reality Plane's opening discipline is unflinching constraint mapping. It distinguishes between fixed constraints and negotiable ones, understands real team capabilities and actual bandwidth (not the roadmap-cartoon version), assesses cost in terms people feel (time, money, energy), and identifies hidden constraints and second-order effects before they bite. The recurring question, stated verbatim in the agent file, is: Is this actually possible in our real context?

Confidence thresholds are how that honesty gets operationalized. MERCURIO pairs each recommendation with a six-band confidence score: 90%+ execute now, 80-90% plan and commit, 70-80% prototype first, 60-70% run a spike, 50-60% gather more data, and below 50% halt. Each band maps to a concrete next action — no band says just wing it. The Wisdom Plane's test is likewise stated in the source: help distinguish between 'hard no' and 'hard but possible.'

In this module you read the pragmatist's own definition of reality, then calibrate one of your own open decisions against the six bands. The win condition is not reaching 90%. It is reaching the band that honestly describes what you know, and then taking the action that band requires.

A 60% confidence score is not a failure — it is a prescription. It says "spike, do not ship." The hazard is not low confidence; the hazard is taking 90%-confidence actions on 60%-confidence evidence.
